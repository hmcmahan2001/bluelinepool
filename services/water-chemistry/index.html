Frequently asked

Pool Water Chemistry Balancing FAQ

Why is water chemistry so important?

Balanced water protects swimmers' skin and eyes, prevents equipment corrosion, extends the life of your plaster and liner, and keeps chlorine working efficiently so you don't over-pay for chemicals.

How often should my water be tested?

Weekly during the swim season (March-October) and every 2 weeks in winter. Chemistry shifts fast with Brazos Valley heat and rain.

Do you test for phosphates?

Yes. Phosphates feed algae, and BCS irrigation water is high in them. We test and treat as part of routine service.

My pool is cloudy — is that a chemistry problem?

Usually yes. Cloudiness is most often a calcium hardness or pH imbalance, sometimes phosphates. We diagnose on-site and fix it.
